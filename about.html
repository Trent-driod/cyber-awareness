About the Cybersecurity & Cybercrime Awareness Program

This initiative is built on community intelligence and risk reduction for traders. Join to gain practical fraud-fighting skills and long-term defense routines.

Understanding the Threat Landscape

The goal is to provide comprehensive awareness training so participants can identify scams, make secure decisions and protect profits. We explore cybersecurity, cybercrime, practical scenarios, and direct actions for traders.

Detailed Topics

  • Cybersecurity definitions and core concepts (CIA triad, threat modeling).
  • Cybercrime categories: payment fraud, identity theft, business email compromise.
  • Business risk outlook: 2026 projections for Ghana and West Africa.
  • Government & industry countermeasures (NCA, banks, telcos).

Support information sources

This curriculum uses verified references, including:

  • National Communications Authority (NCA) incident reports
  • MTN and Vodafone Ghana fraud alerts
  • Local trader surveys and case studies in Accra and Tarkwa
  • Online safety frameworks from the Ghana Cyber Security Authority

MoMo Fraud Deep Dive

Fake reversal calls, OTP-based trickery, and compromised agent accounts are analyzed. Participants receive a fraud flowchart with detection points and escalation steps.

Fake Job Recruitment Scam Analysis

We break down the common narrative, payment methods, and psychological hooks used by scammers. Practice exercises help delegates build rejection scripts and proof-check routines.

Community-source data

We include a crowd-sourced incidents map showing trading districts where reported losses occurred. Traders can contribute via the event feedback form, building a living dataset.

Our event is designed to be a replicable community training model with measurable ROI for business groups.

Extended Community Deliverables

  • Monthly awareness newsletter for traders, including new scam trends and alert alerts.
  • Quarterly group safety review workshop with pre- and post-awareness scoring.
  • Guide to opening an incident case and engaging authorities quickly.
  • Templates for business continuity, secure account checks, and vendor screening.

Practical steps to stay safe

  1. Always verify sender identity before approving payments or sending funds.
  2. Use unique passwords, a credible password manager, and update them regularly.
  3. Report all suspicious communications to NCA, telcos, and local security officers.
  4. Educate your supply chain and customers with role-play and shared checklists.
